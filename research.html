Research Guide

Author(s): Samheart564
Current Version: 1.4.0 | Last Updated: Februrary 18th, 2024


Introduction

Research is one of the most confusing aspects about Azur Lane. This guide hopes to give players a comprehensive overview of the Fleet Technology, Research Academy, and Shipyard tabs of the Lab.


Fleet Technology

Main Article: Fleet Technology Guide


Shipyard

Shipyard UI


This is where you unlock and develop Shipyard ships.


Coin-up

All PR1, PR2/DR2, PR3/DR3, and PR4 ships can have their prints be bought with coins! PR and DR Coin-up are seperate. See this wiki page for more information.


Recommended Shipyard Priority

  • See the Fleetbuilding Guide to see how ships fit into your fleet.


Recommended Development Order:


High Priority:

  • Friedrich der Große

  • Anchorage

Unfortunately, it is very unrealistic to start with the High Priority targets. Therefore, here are other priorities to focus on.


Other Priorities:

  • Coin-up ships.

    • Any PR1, PR2, PR3, and PR4 ship can be enhanced efficiently through Coin-up, making them good to start with.

    • If you need Tech Points for Friedrich der Große, pick Roon. Otherwise, it doesn't matter who is selected.

  • Ships you have a lot of blueprints for.

    • Shipyard ships are useless at lower Development Levels! Therefore, focusing ships you have a lot of blueprints for is best, as you can use them faster compared to other ships.

    • Ships you have selected for your Catch-up fall into this category.

  • Ships for Beginner Rewards.

    • You need 1 ship each from PR1-PR5 due to Beginner Rewards. DR ships count for this as well.


Shipyard Priority by Season:

  • PR1: Roon = Saint Louis >= Monarch > rest

  • PR2: Kitakaze = Gascogne > rest

  • PR3: Cheshire >= Odin > Mainz > rest

  • PR4: Anchorage > August von Parseval >= Marco Polo

  • PR5: Harbin = Chkalov = Prinz Rupprecht

  • PR6: Shimanto = Felix Schultz = Flandre

    • PR6 Catch-up is not released yet.

  • DRs: Friedrich der Große > rest

    • Since FdG has coin-up, she is the highest priority to acquire. Azuma and Drake also have coin-up, but they are worse value compared to FdG.

    • Get all other DRs at your own pace. Since they take forever to max, there is no rush.


Notes:

  • Bolded ships are the recommended Catch-up selections for that season.

    • If a ship is not listed, but has a Catch-up (ex. Drake), do it last.

    • For PR Catch-up Min-Maxing, read Protocore for more information.

Research Academy UI

Research Focus UI


Research Focus and Catch-up are seperate!!!

Notice in the "Research Focus UI" image, "Research Focus" is listed seperately! Read the below sections for more information.


Purpose of Research

To get PR / DR Blueprints to use in Shipyard, as well as Equipment Designs to craft into gear.


Research Focus

When a season is focused, at least 3 out of the 5 available Research Projects will be from that season.


Research Focus Optimization:

Select PR4 or PR5 focus unless you know what you are doing. They have the best ships and gear.

  • PR6 is slightly worse than the options above due to having similar ships but worse gear. Still fine to set as a focus, however.


Why not PR1, PR2, PR3?

  • All the PR ships in PR1, PR2, and PR3 can have their ship blueprints bought with coins directly, instead of having to research for them. PR4 ships can also be bought with coins, but DR4 ships can't, and PR4 gear is good, making PR4 focus fine.

  • All the DR ships in DR2 and DR3 can have their ship blueprints bought with coins directly, instead of having to research for them.

  • All the gear in PR1, PR2, PR3 (and most later seasons) are found in the Prototype Shop and/or the Gear Lab.


Catch-up

When a ship is selected for catch-up, every research you complete will always give a minimum of 1 additional print that is for the catch-up ship selected.


What you get from Catch-up:

  • 300 total (PR) prints for a combination of any of the 6 PR1 ships.

  • 300 total (PR) prints for a combination of any of the 4 PR2 ships.

  • 300 total (PR) prints for a combination of any of the 4 PR3 ships.

  • 300 total (PR) prints for a combination of any of the 3 PR4 ships.

  • 300 total (PR) prints for a combination of any of the 3 PR5 ships.

    • It's recommended that all 300 prints are for the same ship, until the ship reaches Development Level 30.

  • 150 (DR) prints for Friedrich der Große

  • 150 (DR) prints for Azuma

  • 150 (DR) prints for Drake

  • 150 (DR) prints for Ägir

  • 150 (DR) prints for Hakuryuu

You can only do one catch-up at a time, but there is no time limit. It is recommended to do the PR catch-ups before the DR catch-ups.


Recommended Catch-up selections: See this section for more information.

Optimal Research Academy Projects

Notes:

  • Projects ARE rated within a tier!

  • For D researches, Rainbow Ship Blueprints > Gold Ship Blueprints, regardless of the research length / rarity color.

  • Only spend cubes on research if you have a large (400+ cubes or higher) surplus, and are willing to risk your gacha chances.

  • Avoid taking H-type or D-type projects once all ships in that season are maxed. The protocore gained is not worth it.

  • Always have a research running at all times, even if it's bad!

PR Catch-up Min-Maxing

  • This is NOT necessary. You will be fine if you don't do this.

This method involves manipulating Research to allow you to convert all the Catch-up prints from a season into Prototype Core. This is only recommended for PR1 and PR2. Do NOT do this with any of the DR Catch-ups!

  1. Set your Catch-up to PR4, PR5, or any DR ship.

    • See Shipyard Priority for more information.

  2. Research a PR ship that is of the same season that you want to convert Catch-up prints for. Any PR ship works.

  3. While your valuable (PR4, PR5, DR) Catch-up prints are slowly trickling in, obtain and develop the ship from Step 2 using coins until the ship is development level 30. This entire process should take about 50 days or less, assuming you are spending 3000 Coins daily.

    • After the ship is obtained, feel free to go back and develop a ship from a different, less-valuable (PR1, PR2) Catch-up season, or a different ship from any season. Always have a ship being developed at all times!

  4. After the ship from Step 2 is Development Level 30, set your Catch-up to that ship.

    • If you are not finished with the valuable (PR4, PR5, DR) Catch-up prints, it is fine to remain on the valuable Catch-up and come back to this step later.

    • You do NOT need to focus on Fate Simulation for this ship.

  5. Convert all prints of that ship into Prototype Core in the Prototype Shop. This should take about 2 months to convert all the prints.

    • While these prints are trickling in, feel free to continue developing more PR/DR ships on the side.

  6. Repeat these steps until all desired Catch-up prints are converted into Prototype Core.


Optimizing Protocore

Ideally, the total Protocore income is 5000 from the methods listed above.

  • High Priority Purchases (4125 ): Triple 406mm/50 Main Gun Mount (x3), Prototype Tenrai.

  • With the rest of your currency, you can consider buying some of the other gear listed above.
